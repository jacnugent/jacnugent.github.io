About Me

I am a third-year graduate student at the University of Washington studying atmospheric sciences.
My research focuses on the simulation of deep convection and cirrus clouds in the Tropical Tropopause Layer in global storm-resolving models.
I am advised by Professor Chris Bretherton and am a member of the NSF PIRE-supported International Partnership for Cirrus Studies.
Currently I am working as a Research Assistant at Vulcan Climate Modeling. This site is a work in progress, so check back for updates!

Research Interests

Deep Convection

Cloud Microphysics

High-Resolution Modeling

Machine Learning
